URSA MAJOR

AKA: The Great Bear

Brightest Star: Alioth, magnitude 1.76

Location: Northern Hemisphere

Visibility: It is a circumpolar constellation for many in the Northern Hemisphere, meaning it never sets below the horizon and is always visible. 

Story: Ursa Major is associated with various myths, most famously the Greek myth of Callisto, who was transformed into a bear and placed in the sky by Zeus.
